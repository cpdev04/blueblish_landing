FAQ

What causes prostate enlargement ?
Prostate enlargement, also known as benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH), is primarily caused by age-related hormonal changes. As men age, the prostate gland often enlarges, leading to urinary symptoms.
What are the symptoms, and when should I seek treatment?
Symptoms of prostate enlargement include frequent urination, difficulty starting or maintaining a urine stream, weak urine flow, and the sensation of incomplete bladder emptying. If you experience any of these symptoms, especially if they interfere with daily life, it's advisable to seek medical evaluation.
What treatment options does BlueBliss Hospital offer ?
BlueBliss Hospital offers a range of treatment options for prostate enlargement, including medications to alleviate symptoms, minimally invasive procedures like UroLift or Rezūm therapy, and surgical interventions such as transurethral resection of the prostate (TURP).
What's the recovery process after treatment?
Recovery from prostate enlargement treatment varies depending on the chosen procedure. Generally, patients may experience some temporary discomfort, urinary urgency, or mild bleeding. Most can resume normal activities within a few days to weeks, with full recovery expected in a few weeks to months.
Are there any risks associated with treatment ?
While treatments for prostate enlargement are generally safe, they carry potential risks and complications. These can include urinary incontinence, erectile dysfunction, retrograde ejaculation, and in rare cases, urinary tract infection or bleeding. Your healthcare provider will discuss these risks and help you make an informed decision about treatment.
